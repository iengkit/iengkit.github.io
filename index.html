Ieng Kit Ho (Nicholas)

In December 2016 I will be completing my B.Sc. at the University of British Columbia, majoring in Computer Science and Statistics. My interests are in Machine Learning and Scientific Computing.

In summer 2016 I worked on a NSERC project in methods of approximation theory for a wide range of applications in physics, chemistry and engineering under the supervision of a faculty emeritus from the UBC Institute of Applied Mathematics (IAM). In this research paper, we proposed a high-precision pseudospectral algorithm based on nonclassical orthogonal polynomials for solving nonlinear variational problems and more essentially for determining the wave function and the energy of a quantum many-body system.

I am currently developing a hybrid parallel Coppersmith-Winograd algorithm for high speed matrix multiplication and the goal is to attain a better runtime than most of the existing algorithms for matrix multiplication (such as the traditional IJK algorithm) when {N,M,K} is large (i.e. A and B are matrices with dimensions NxM and MxK respectively). I am also building deep learning models (RNN and LSTM) for financial prediction and classification with python using TensorFlow.

In my spare time I enjoy solving Project Euler problems. I am a passionate problem solver! :-)
