Ieng Kit Ho (Nicholas)

In December 2016 I completed my B.Sc. at the University of British Columbia, majoring in Computer Science and Statistics. My interests are in Software Development, Machine Learning, Computer Vision and Scientific Computing.

In summer 2016 I worked on a NSERC research project under the supervision of an UBC Institute of Applied Mathematics (IAM) faculty emeritus in numerical methods of Approximation Theory for applications in physics, chemistry and engineering. We introduced a pseudospectral algorithm based on nonclassical orthogonal polynomials to approximate the quantum mechanical radial wavefunction in DFT (Density Functional Theory).

The paper can be found here

In my spare time, I enjoy solving Project Euler problems! :-)

Industrial Experience: In summer 2015, I worked as a Core Infrastructure Engineer (intern) for the second-largest commercial banking group in Hong Kong.
